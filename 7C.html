Chapter 7 Exercise C

Posted on July 3, 2016 by Mohammad Rashidi

2. Suppose $T$ is a positive operator on $V$. Suppose $v,w\in V$ are such that \[Tv=w\quad\text{ and } \quad Tw=v.\] Prove that $v=w$.

Solution: Note that $T$ is a positive operator on $V$, we have \begin{equation}\label{7CP2.1} \langle T(v-w),v-w\rangle\ge 0. \end{equation}On the other hand, \[Tv=w\quad\text{ and } \quad Tw=v\]imply that $T(v-w)=w-v$, hence \begin{equation}\label{7CP2.2}\langle T(v-w),v-w\rangle=-\langle v-w,v-w\rangle\le 0.\end{equation}Therefore $\langle v-w,v-w\rangle=0$ by (\ref{7CP2.1}) and (\ref{7CP2.2}), i.e. $v=w$.
